Catalan Castellers

The Towers the People Built

A castelle in Catalan means, simply, a castle. The primary difference image that you should understand is that these castles are not made of stone but people! This is a tradition that reaches back to 18th century Catalunya and is now a “Masterpiece of the Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity” by Unesco which effectively means that these are recognized by Catalan People as an integral part of their cultural identity, providing a sense of continuity, social cohesion, and solidarity.

The roots of this activity are said to come from Catalan farmers from the city of Valles when good weather came as a spectacle. When they moved to other village. At each village the villagers were said to add to the towers creating one unified and joint community. Now, Modern Castelleres participate in competitions surrounded around building these towers to pay homage to their unifying roots.

Much like the Sardana, Castelleres are a symbol of Catalan unity. The strong individuals at the base of the tower support those smaller members that climb the towers. Even children get involved with these events showing that every member of Catalan society has a place within their unity.
